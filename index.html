Course Overview

CIS 3990 introduces students to the fundamental principles of Artificial Intelligence (AI), covering theoretical foundations and practical applications.

Topics include:

  • AI Philosophy, Sci-Fi, and Real-World Applications
  • Large Language Models (LLMs) & Neural Networks
  • K-Nearest Neighbors, Vector Representations, and Embeddings
  • Robotic Agents
  • Search Algorithms: Uninformed and Informed Search
  • Classical Planning and Logic-Based Representations
  • Markov Decision Processes (MDPs) & Reasoning Under Uncertainty
  • Reinforcement Learning: Theory and Applications
  • AI Ethics and Societal Impacts

This course is designed for freshman or sophomore students who plan to major in Artificial Intelligence (AI). It serves as one of the introductory courses in the AI curriculum alongside ESE 2000. CIS 3990 complements ESE 2000 by focusing on language models, robotics, and search algorithms, whereas ESE 2000 emphasizes machine learning, neural networks, and dynamical systems. Together, they provide a comprehensive foundation in AI.
